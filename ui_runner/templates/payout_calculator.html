🎃 How Goblin Lines Work

Goblins move the line in your favor. They reduce the payout multiplier because they're easier to hit. The further the line moves from standard, the bigger the payout reduction. Key metric: played ÷ standard = delta%. A goblin at 90% of standard is priced very differently than one at 60%.

Example: Standard = 20.5. Goblin at 16.5 → delta% = 80.5%. Goblin at 12.0 → delta% = 58.5%.
Same 3-leg base of 6x, but PP prices the 58.5% goblin much lower because it's far more likely to hit.

Key insight: Log both lines every time — the label "Goblin" tells you nothing without knowing how far the line moved.
😈 How Demon Lines Work

Demons move the line against you. They increase the payout multiplier. Delta% for Demons will be above 1.0 — a demon at 110% of standard is shallow; one at 140%+ is extreme. The boost varies widely and is not linear.

Example: Standard = 24.5. Demon at 26.5 → delta% = 108%. Demon at 34.5 → delta% = 141%.
PP captures extra margin on Demon lines — log your observations to see if the boost is worth the harder line.

Key insight: Demon boosts are often not proportional to actual difficulty. Your logged data will reveal whether PP under- or over-prices specific delta% ranges.
🧮 Mixed Slip Payout Logic
PrizePicks does not publish the formula for how Goblin/Demon legs adjust the slip multiplier. Based on observations, the mechanics appear to work roughly like this:

1. Base multiplier = standard payout for the leg count (e.g. 6x for 3 legs)
2. Per-leg adjustment = each Goblin/Demon applies a factor based on its delta%, not just its label
3. Goblin factor ≈ reduces payout, scales with how far below standard the line is
4. Demon factor ≈ increases payout, scales with how far above standard the line is
5. Factors multiply together — one Goblin and one Demon partially offset each other

The exact per-leg factor is dynamic per-prop and undisclosed. Logging real delta% observations is the only way to reverse-engineer the actual curve.
